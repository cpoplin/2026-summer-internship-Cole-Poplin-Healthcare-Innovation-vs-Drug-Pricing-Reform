**2026 Summer Internship Program**
**Market Intelligence Research Case Study**

## Project Overview

Biotech and pharma are caught between transformative innovation (gene therapy, AI discovery) and political pressure to cap prices. This project maps which sub-sectors survive regulatory headwinds and where the next decade of healthcare alpha lives.

Currently, the **Lower/Bronze** tier (core data engine and plotting) and the **Higher/Silver** tier (interactive Streamlit application) are provided.

## Data Strategy & Sub-Sector Basket Approach

Pure sub-sector data is not directly available via the `yfinance` API (or any other public API for that matter). To overcome this challenge, sub-sector performance is extrapolated by tracking baskets of representative target companies (though notably large pharma is inherently diversified):

*   **Small Molecules (Traditional Pharma)**: Bristol-Myers Squibb (`BMY`), Pfizer (`PFE`), AbbVie (`ABBV`).
*   **Complex Biologics (Innovation Leaders)**: Amgen (`AMGN`), Regeneron (`REGN`), Novo Nordisk (`NVO`).

## Tier Details

### Lower/Bronze — Statistical & Trend Analysis
*   Loads monthly stock data from Yahoo Finance via `yfinance` from 1995 to 2026.
*   Highlights two critical regulatory/market turmoil windows:
    *   **The Patent Cliff (2000–2005)**: Initiated by the Hatch-Waxman Act, triggering heavy pressures on small-molecule traditional drugs.
    *   **The Inflation Reduction Act (2022–2025)**: Price-capping drug pricing reform pressures.
*   Normalizes prices to a base of 100 at the start of each turmoil window to compare relative performance (seen in correlation_graph).
*   Generates 5 publication-quality charts under `./charts/`.
*   Computes overall returns and the alpha spread between Complex Biologics and Small Molecules.

### Higher/Silver — Interactive Analysis Dashboard
*   **Dynamic Customization**: Interactive multiselect and free-form comma-separated inputs in the sidebar allow users to add/remove representative tickers in the Small Molecule and Complex Biologics baskets.
*   **Macro-Date Sliders**: A range slider filters start/end years and dynamically recalculates CAGR, returns, and net alpha spreads.
*   **Page 1: Context & Historical Trajectories (Subsector Showdown)**: Dynamic line charts of normalized subsector index performance with metrics KPI cards.
*   **Page 2: Stress-Test Events & Risk**: Runs event studies (Prozac Patent Loss and CMS Price Negotiations) with volatility metrics, plus a corporate stock collapse graph representing drawdowns across 136 companies.
*   **Page 3: Thesis Verdict & Conclusion**: YoY return bar charts, custom HTML Verdict dashboard, and a sensitivity grid displaying outcomes across start dates.

## Limitations

*   **Basket Representation**: Tracking 3 companies per basket represents large, diversified firms which might not isolate pure subsector trends perfectly.
*   **Dividend Adjustments**: Returns are calculated on split-adjusted closing prices and do not fully reflect dividend reinvestment payouts.
*   **Historical Precedent**: Past performance under regulatory pressures may not predict future reactions as the healthcare landscape evolves.
